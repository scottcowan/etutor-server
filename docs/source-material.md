## A Fire Upon the Deep — Vernor Vinge

**Calibre library:** 192.168.0.25:8083 / Calibre Library
**Search:** "fire upon the deep"

### Why it's relevant

Vinge's novel introduces the **Zones of Thought** — a physical cosmology
where the laws of physics (and therefore intelligence) vary by location in
the galaxy:

| Zone | AI capability |
|---|---|
| The Unthinking Depths | No complex computation possible |
| The Slow Zone | Human-level intelligence is the ceiling — FTL impossible |
| The Beyond | Superintelligence and FTL both possible |
| The Transcend | Incomprehensible godlike Powers |

Earth is in the Slow Zone. The novel's conflict partly turns on what
happens when Slow Zone constraints meet Beyond-level intelligence.

### The "Slow Zone" parallel for eTutor

The cyberdeck running a 3B model locally is operating in the Slow Zone
by design — constrained, deliberate, offline-capable. The cloud (Claude
Sonnet) is the Beyond. The cyberharness router is the ship navigating
between zones.

This framing is useful for:
- Explaining the local/cloud routing decision to technically curious children (10+)
- Motivating why the device works offline ("it carries its own intelligence")
- A discussion prompt: "What would it mean for a computer to be *too smart* to run here?"

### Curriculum hooks

- **Physics:** how do physical laws constrain what's possible? (Zones as analogy)
- **Computing:** what limits how fast a computer can think?
- **Philosophy/ethics:** the Powers in the Transcend — should intelligence have limits?
- **Storytelling:** alien communication (Tines pack-mind) — how do different minds talk to each other?

### The Tines — pack mind relevance

The Tines are aliens who think as a collective — individual members of a
pack share one mind via ultrasonic communication. Separating a pack member
degrades intelligence; adding members changes personality. A rich discussion
prompt for children thinking about what "thinking" means.
